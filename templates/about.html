Tea leaf, also known as Camellia sinensis, is the primary plant used in the production of tea. The leaves of the tea plant are harvested and processed to create various types of tea, including green tea, black tea, oolong tea, and white tea. Tea leaves contain compounds such as catechins, flavonoids, and caffeine, which contribute to the taste, aroma, and potential health benefits of processing techniques tea.

Tea leaves are cultivated in tea plantations and undergo different processing methods, including withering, rolling, oxidation, and drying, to achieve the desired characteristics of the final tea product. The quality and flavor of tea depend on factors such as the tea variety, growing conditions, processing techniques, and the presence of any diseases or pests that may affect the health of the tea plants.
