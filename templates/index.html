ABOUT US

There are several types of tea leaf diseases that can affect tea plants, including blister blight, grey blight, red rust, brown blight, and pink disease. Blister blight is one of the most common tea leaf diseases, and it is caused by the fungus Exobasidium vexans. This disease affects the leaves of the tea plant, causing them to form blisters and eventually turn brown and die. Grey blight, also known as Ascochyta blight, is caused by the fungus Ascochyta species. It affects the tea plant's stems, causing them to turn brown and develop lesions. Red rust is caused by the fungus Cephaleuros virescens and appears as red spots on the leaves of the tea plant. Brown blight, caused by the fungus Pestalotiopsis species, affects the tea plant's leaves and can cause them to become brown and brittle. Pink disease, caused by the bacterium Acetobacter species, can affect the bark and wood of the tea plant, causing it to decay.
